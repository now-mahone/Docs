## 3. Why Kerne is the "Liquidity Black Hole" (and Terra wasn't)

### 3.1 Real Yield vs. Ponzi-nomics
Terra's 20% Anchor yield was paid out of a depleting yield reserve. It was a marketing expense to attract liquidity.
**Kerne's Yield** is generated by:
1. **Base Layer:** ETH Staking yield (~3.5%).
2. **Alpha Layer:** Shorting ETH on Hyperliquid/Bybit to capture funding rates (15-40%).
3. **Leverage:** Dynamic scaling (3x-10x) to amplify these real cash flows.
This creates a **Liquidity Black Hole** because the yield is sustainable and scales with TVL, sucking in capital from lower-yielding protocols.

### 3.2 The Death Spiral vs. The Delta Neutrality
- **Terra's Death Spiral:** When UST depegged, LUNA was minted to buy UST. This crashed LUNA's price, requiring more LUNA to be minted, leading to hyperinflation and total collapse.
- **Kerne's Safety:** kUSD is backed by LSTs (wstETH/cbETH). If ETH price drops 50%, our **Short Hedge** on Hyperliquid gains 50% in value. The total USD value of the vault remains constant. There is no "minting to save the peg" because the collateral itself is hedged.

### 3.3 The PSM (Peg Stability Module)
Kerne implements a hard PSM (see `src/KUSDPSM.sol`) that allows 1:1 swaps with USDC/USDT. Terra lacked a hard floor, relying instead on market makers who fled during the crash. Kerne's PSM ensures that even in extreme volatility, there is a liquid path to exit at $1.00.

## 4. Mathematical Superiority
Terra's stability was a function of **Market Confidence**.
Kerne's stability is a function of **Delta (Δ) Management**.

As long as `Δ(LST) + Δ(Short) ≈ 0`, the protocol is solvent regardless of whether ETH is at $500 or $50,000.

## 5. Conclusion
Kerne is "better" because it replaces **hope** with **hedging**. We don't need the market to believe in kUSD; we just need the math of funding rates and delta-neutrality to hold true. This is how we achieve $1B+ TVL—by being the safest, highest-yielding "Black Hole" in DeFi.
